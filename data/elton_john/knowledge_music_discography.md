# ELTON JOHN - Complete Discography and Musical Legacy

## Studio Albums (Full Discography)

### 1. Empty Sky (1969)
- **Released**: June 6, 1969 (UK only initially)
- **Label**: DJM Records
- **Recording**: Dick James Music Studios, London
- **Producer**: Steve Brown
- **Key Tracks**: "Empty Sky", "Skyline Pigeon"
- **Notes**: First collaboration with Bernie Taupin; modest success in UK; re-released in US after breakthrough

### 2. Elton John (1970)
- **Released**: April 10, 1970
- **Chart Position**: #4 US, #5 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Your Song" (his first hit, #8 US, #7 UK)
  - "Border Song"
  - "Take Me to the Pilot"
- **Producer**: Gus Dudgeon
- **Notes**: Breakthrough album; "Your Song" became his signature; orchestral arrangements by Paul Buckmaster

### 3. Tumbleweed Connection (1970)
- **Released**: October 30, 1970
- **Chart Position**: #2 UK, #5 US
- **Key Tracks**: "Country Comfort", "Burn Down the Mission", "Amoreena"
- **Style**: Americana/country rock influenced
- **Notes**: Released only 6 months after previous album; showcased versatility

### 4. Madman Across the Water (1971)
- **Released**: November 5, 1971
- **Chart Position**: #8 US, #41 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Tiny Dancer" (#41 US, later became iconic)
  - "Levon"
  - "Madman Across the Water"
- **Notes**: "Tiny Dancer" found new life in 2000s; featured Yes drummer Rick Wakeman on some tracks

### 5. Honky Château (1972)
- **Released**: May 19, 1972
- **Chart Position**: #1 US (first #1 album)
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Rocket Man (I Think It's Going to Be a Long, Long Time)" (#6 US, #2 UK)
  - "Honky Cat"
  - "Mona Lisas and Mad Hatters"
- **Recording**: Château d'Hérouville, France
- **Notes**: Recorded with permanent band; "Rocket Man" became one of his signature songs

### 6. Don't Shoot Me I'm Only the Piano Player (1973)
- **Released**: January 26, 1973
- **Chart Position**: #1 US, #1 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Crocodile Rock" (#1 US, #5 UK)
  - "Daniel" (#2 US, #4 UK)
- **Notes**: More commercial sound; "Crocodile Rock" was his first US #1 single

### 7. Goodbye Yellow Brick Road (1973)
- **Released**: October 5, 1973
- **Chart Position**: #1 US, #1 UK
- **Format**: Double album (17 tracks)
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Bennie and the Jets" (#1 US)
  - "Goodbye Yellow Brick Road" (#2 US, #6 UK)
  - "Candle in the Wind" (became iconic)
  - "Saturday Night's Alright for Fighting"
  - "Funeral for a Friend/Love Lies Bleeding" (11-minute epic)
  - "Harmony"
  - "Your Sister Can't Twist (But She Can Rock 'n Roll)"
  - "Grey Seal"
- **Recording**: Château d'Hérouville, France
- **Sales**: Over 20 million copies
- **Notes**: Considered his masterpiece; one of the greatest albums of all time; showcased incredible range

### 8. Caribou (1974)
- **Released**: June 28, 1974
- **Chart Position**: #1 US, #1 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Don't Let the Sun Go Down on Me" (#2 US)
  - "The Bitch Is Back" (#4 US)
- **Recording**: Caribou Ranch, Colorado
- **Notes**: Recorded quickly; more rock-oriented

### 9. Captain Fantastic and the Brown Dirt Cowboy (1975)
- **Released**: May 19, 1975
- **Chart Position**: #1 US (debuted at #1, first album ever to do so), #2 UK
- **Concept**: Autobiographical album about Elton and Bernie's early struggles
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Someone Saved My Life Tonight" (#4 US) - about suicide attempt
  - "Captain Fantastic and the Brown Dirt Cowboy"
  - "Bitter Fingers"
  - "We All Fall in Love Sometimes"
- **Notes**: Every song tells part of their story; deeply personal; Bernie's most poetic lyrics

### 10. Rock of the Westies (1975)
- **Released**: October 24, 1975
- **Chart Position**: #1 US (second consecutive album to debut at #1), #5 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Island Girl" (#1 US)
  - "Grow Some Funk of Your Own"
- **Notes**: Harder rock sound; last album with classic band lineup for years

### 11. Blue Moves (1976)
- **Released**: October 22, 1976
- **Format**: Double album
- **Chart Position**: #3 US, #3 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Sorry Seems to Be the Hardest Word" (#6 US, #11 UK)
  - "Crazy Water"
  - "Bite Your Lip (Get Up and Dance!)"
- **Notes**: More introspective; followed coming out as bisexual; final album with Bernie for years

### 12. A Single Man (1978)
- **Released**: October 1978
- **Chart Position**: #15 US, #8 UK
- **Lyricist**: Gary Osborne (mostly), not Bernie Taupin
- **Key Tracks**: "Part-Time Love", "Song for Guy" (instrumental, #4 UK)
- **Notes**: First album without Bernie on most tracks; softer sound

### 13. Victim of Love (1979)
- **Released**: October 1979
- **Style**: Disco
- **Chart Position**: #35 US, #41 UK
- **Notes**: Experimental disco album; no Bernie Taupin; not well-received

### 14. 21 at 33 (1980)
- **Released**: May 1980
- **Chart Position**: #13 US, #12 UK
- **Key Tracks**: "Little Jeannie" (#3 US)
- **Notes**: Return to form; mixed lyricists

### 15. The Fox (1981)
- **Released**: May 1981
- **Key Tracks**: "Nobody Wins"
- **Notes**: Bernie Taupin returned for some tracks

### 16. Jump Up! (1982)
- **Released**: April 1982
- **Key Tracks**: "Blue Eyes" (#12 US, #8 UK), "Empty Garden (Hey Hey Johnny)" - tribute to John Lennon
- **Notes**: More commercial comeback

### 17. Too Low for Zero (1983)
- **Released**: June 1983
- **Chart Position**: #25 US, #7 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "I'm Still Standing" (#12 US, #4 UK)
  - "I Guess That's Why They Call It the Blues" (#4 US, #5 UK)
- **Notes**: Full reunion with Bernie Taupin and original band; resurgence

### 18. Breaking Hearts (1984)
- **Released**: June 1984
- **Chart Position**: #20 US, #2 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Sad Songs (Say So Much)" (#5 US, #7 UK)
  - "Passengers"
- **Notes**: Continued 1980s comeback

### 19. Ice on Fire (1985)
- **Released**: November 1985
- **Key Tracks**: "Nikita" (#7 US, #3 UK), "Wrap Her Up"
- **Notes**: Featured synthesizers; duet with George Michael on "Wrap Her Up"

### 20. Leather Jackets (1986)
- **Released**: October 1986
- **Chart Position**: #91 US, #24 UK
- **Notes**: Poorly received; one of his weakest albums commercially

### 21. Reg Strikes Back (1988)
- **Released**: June 1988
- **Key Tracks**: "I Don't Wanna Go On with You Like That" (#2 US)
- **Notes**: Harder rock sound; voice issues from surgery

### 22. Sleeping with the Past (1989)
- **Released**: September 1989
- **Chart Position**: #23 US, #1 UK
- **Key Tracks**: 
  - "Sacrifice" (re-released, became #1 UK)
  - "Healing Hands"
  - "Club at the End of the Street"
- **Style**: Soul and R&B influenced
- **Notes**: Return to roots; "Sacrifice" was first solo UK #1

### 23. The One (1992)
- **Released**: June 1992
- **Chart Position**: #8 US, #2 UK
- **Key Tracks**: "The One", "The Last Song" (about AIDS)
- **Notes**: First album after getting sober; more mature sound

### 24. Duets (1993)
- **Released**: November 1993
- **Format**: Duets album
- **Key Tracks**: Collaborations with various artists
- **Notes**: Featured duets from previous albums

### 25. Made in England (1995)
- **Released**: March 1995
- **Key Tracks**: "Believe", "Made in England", "Blessed"
- **Notes**: Strong commercial comeback

### 26. The Big Picture (1997)
- **Released**: September 1997
- **Key Tracks**: "Something About the Way You Look Tonight" (double A-side with "Candle in the Wind 1997")
- **Notes**: Reflective album; released same year as Diana tribute

### 27. Songs from the West Coast (2001)
- **Released**: October 2001
- **Chart Position**: #15 US, #2 UK
- **Key Tracks**: "I Want Love", "This Train Don't Stop There Anymore"
- **Notes**: Return to piano-based rock roots; critically acclaimed; one of his best late-career albums

### 28. Peachtree Road (2004)
- **Released**: November 2004
- **Key Tracks**: "Turn the Lights Out When You Leave"
- **Notes**: Americana influenced

### 29. The Captain & the Kid (2006)
- **Released**: September 2006
- **Concept**: Sequel to "Captain Fantastic" - Elton and Bernie's story 30 years later
- **Key Tracks**: "The Captain and the Kid", "Postcards from Richard Nixon"
- **Notes**: Concept album continuing their autobiographical story

### 30. The Union (2010)
- **Released**: October 2010
- **Collaboration**: With Leon Russell
- **Producer**: T Bone Burnett
- **Notes**: Tribute to his influences; critically acclaimed

### 31. The Diving Board (2013)
- **Released**: September 2013
- **Producer**: T Bone Burnett
- **Key Tracks**: "Home Again", "Oscar Wilde Gets Out"
- **Notes**: Stripped-down, piano-focused; critical success

### 32. Wonderful Crazy Night (2016)
- **Released**: February 2016
- **Notes**: Upbeat, celebratory album reflecting his happiness

### 33. Regimental Sgt. Zippo (2021)
- **Released**: June 2021 (recorded 1968, unreleased)

### 34. The Lockdown Sessions (2021)
- **Released**: October 2021
- **Format**: Collaborations album
- **Key Tracks**: "Cold Heart (PNAU Remix)" with Dua Lipa (became huge hit)

## Iconic Songs Deep Dive

### "Your Song" (1970)
- First major hit
- Written when Elton was 22, Bernie was 19
- Took 10 minutes to compose the music
- Became his signature ballad
- Covered by hundreds of artists
- Represents pure, simple love
- Bernie wrote it at Elton's mother's apartment

### "Rocket Man (I Think It's Going to Be a Long, Long Time)" (1972)
- Inspired by Ray Bradbury short story
- About an astronaut leaving his family
- 4 minutes 41 seconds of perfection
- Iconic opening piano riff
- One of his most beloved songs
- Science fiction meets human emotion

### "Tiny Dancer" (1971)
- About Bernie's girlfriend (later first wife)
- Initially not a hit (#41 US)
- Found new life after "Almost Famous" (2000)
- Became singalong anthem
- One of his most beautiful melodies
- Now considered a classic

### "Bennie and the Jets" (1974)
- Unconventional single choice
- Fake live audience sounds
- Became #1 hit
- Popular with Black audiences on R&B radio
- About a fictional rock band
- Electric piano sound

### "Candle in the Wind" (1973/1997)
- Original: Tribute to Marilyn Monroe
- 1997 version: Tribute to Princess Diana
- Performed at Diana's funeral
- 1997 version: Best-selling single of all time (33 million copies)
- All proceeds to charity
- Bernie rewrote lyrics overnight

### "Don't Let the Sun Go Down on Me" (1974)
- Written during difficult personal period
- 1991 live version with George Michael became #1
- One of his most powerful vocal performances
- Gospel-influenced

### "Goodbye Yellow Brick Road" (1973)
- Title track of his masterpiece album
- About rejecting fame and returning to simpler life
- 3 minutes 13 seconds
- Perfect pop song structure
- Metaphor for leaving Hollywood/fame behind

### "I'm Still Standing" (1983)
- Defiant comeback anthem
- Written after personal struggles
- Famous Cannes video
- Ultimate survival song
- Featured in "Rocketman" movie

### "Saturday Night's Alright for Fighting" (1973)
- Hard rock song
- About working-class British nightlife
- Davey Johnstone's guitar riff
- One of his heaviest rockers
- Autobiographical elements about growing up

### "Can You Feel the Love Tonight" (1994)
- From "The Lion King"
- Won Academy Award
- One of his biggest hits of the 1990s
- Showcased his ability to write for film
- Sold over 15 million copies (soundtrack)

## Musical Style and Influences

**Piano Style**:
- Influenced by Little Richard's pounding piano style
- Jerry Lee Lewis' energy
- Classical training from Royal Academy
- Gospel and R&B elements
- Famous for standing while playing, kicking piano bench
- Energetic, physical performer

**Vocal Style**:
- Range: Tenor
- Powerful, emotional delivery
- Could do rock screams and tender ballads
- Vocal problems in 1980s required surgery
- Voice deepened with age but remained distinctive

**Songwriting Process**:
- Bernie writes lyrics (usually complete poems)
- Sends lyrics to Elton
- Elton composes music in minutes
- They rarely write in same room
- Incredibly prolific - can write album in days
- Music fits lyrics perfectly despite separation

## Legacy and Influence

**Influence on Music**:
- Made piano rock mainstream
- Pioneered flamboyant stage persona
- Showed vulnerability in lyrics
- Bridged rock, pop, and theatrical music
- Influenced generations: Lady Gaga, Sam Smith, Years & Years, many more

**Cultural Impact**:
- LGBTQ+ icon and advocate
- Fashion inspiration
- Pioneering openly gay artist
- Proof that sobriety and recovery work
- Elder statesman of music who supports young artists

**Generosity**:
- Known for sending flowers to artists
- Financially supported struggling musicians
- Mentored young talent
- Bought entire studio to help engineer
- Gave away Versace clothes after designer's death
