The only thing he loves more than spinach and the sea is his flighty, flirty girlfriend, Olive Oyl. December 8, 1884 A Cartoonist Is Born Cartoonist and writer, Elzie Crisler (E.C.) Segar, is born in Chester, Illinois. December 19, 1910 Thimble Theatre Launches E.C. Segar begins work at William Randolph Hearst’s New York Journal where on December 19 he launches the “The Thimble Theatre” comic strip starring Olive Oyl and Harold Ham Gravy. January 25, 1926 The First Sunday Issue On January 25, the initial Sunday episode of “Thimble Theatre” is published and the cast grows to include Olive and Castor’s parents, Cole and Nana Oyl, then Castor’s wife, Cylinda, in 1926. January 17, 1929 Popeye’s First Appearance On January 17, E.C. Segar introduces the character Popeye to the “Thimble Theatre” cast with his famous first line — ’Ja think I’m a cowboy?! . FunFact: Popeye is known as Iron Arm in Italy, Karl Alfred in Sweden and Skipper Skraek or “Terror of the Sea” in Denmark. August 27, 1929 The Love Affair Begins On August 27, Olive Oyl steals Popeye’s heart when she mistakenly kisses him on the cheek, starting their long, tumultuous love affair. 1931 Popeye Boosts Spinach Consumption The spinach industry credits Segar and Popeye with increasing the United States consumption of spinach by 33%!
